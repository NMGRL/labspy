Pychron

Pychron is a comprehensive hardware control and data reduction package developed and maintained by Jake Ross of the New Mexico Geochronology Research Laboratory. Written in Python2.7 Pychron interfaces with the digital electronics supplied by MAP via the GPIB interface (IEEE-488) to control and/or monitor all of the critical mass spectrometer components. The Pychron software is also interfaced (GPIB and RS-232) to most of the extraction system. The Pychron spectrometer and extraction system control features allow the NMGRL to run completely automated for several days at a time, with little or no human interaction.

After data is collected by the Pychron program, it is stored in a MySQL database accessible to all staff and students of the NMGRL. The data reduction features of the program allow unsurpassed control in handling of background, baseline, regression, discrimination, J-value and irradiation constant values, statistics and their application to a sample's 40Ar/39Ar apparent age. Manuscript-quality graphs and tables are easily generated and modified by the Pychron software.

Without the Pychron software package, the NMGRL would not be capable of producing the quality or quantity of data that it does.
